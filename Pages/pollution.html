What is ocean pollution?

Ocean pollution refers to the introduction of harmful substances and waste into the ocean that negatively affect marine ecosystems, wildlife, and even human health. These pollutants come from many sources, including plastic trash, chemicals, oil spills, sewage, and excess nutrients. Once in the ocean, these contaminants can damage coral reefs, poison marine animals, disrupt food chains, and degrade the overall health of the ocean — which is vital to life on Earth.

Types of pollutants
  • Plastic Pollution
  • Chemical Pollution
  • Oil Pollution
  • Nutrient Pollution
  • Sewage and Wastewater
  • Heavy Metals and Radioactive Waste
How you can help

  • Reduce Single-Use Plastics

    Plastic pollution is one of the biggest threats to our oceans. Swap out disposable plastic items (like bags, bottles, and straws) for reusable alternatives. Every plastic item you skip helps reduce the plastic ending up in waterways.

  • Recyle Properly

    Make sure you're recycling the right way. Clean your recyclables, follow local guidelines, and avoid "wish-cycling" — putting non-recyclables in the bin hoping they’ll get sorted later.

  • Choose Eco-Friendly Products

    Use natural cleaning products, avoid microbeads in personal care items, and choose products labeled as biodegradable or ocean-safe to keep harmful chemicals out of water systems.

  • Support Ocean-Friendly Brands & Initiatives

    Support businesses and nonprofits working to clean up marine pollution or reduce their environmental impact. Your spending choices can push industries toward more sustainable practices.

  • Join (or Organize) a Cleanup

    Beach, river, and neighborhood cleanups remove trash before it reaches the ocean. Look for local events — or create your own!
